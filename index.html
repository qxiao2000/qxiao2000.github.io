About

I am a PhD candidate in Robotics at Georgia Institute of Technology, advised by Prof. Matthew Gombolay. My research focuses on probabilistic state estimation and factor-graph inference for real-time robotic systems.

I have applied factor-graph state estimation to multi-camera object tracking, dynamics estimation and prediction, cuboid estimation for autonomous driving, and vision-based robot localization using tennis courtline geometry. I have experience with asynchronous inference using incremental GTSAM solvers and benchmarking against classical baselines (e.g., EKF).

Previously, I received my M.S. in Mechanical Engineering and Materials Science from Duke University (2018) and my B.S. in Energy and Power Engineering from North China Electric Power University (2016).

Research

My research focuses on probabilistic state estimation and factor-graph inference for real-time robotic systems. Specific areas include:

  • Multi-camera object tracking and localization
  • Dynamics estimation and prediction for fast-moving objects
  • Factor-graph-based state estimation with GTSAM
  • Vision-based robot state estimation using geometric landmarks
  • End-to-end system integration with ROS/ROS2
High-Accuracy Agile Robot Localization with Multi-Camera Line Landmarks
Built an asynchronous robot pose estimator using probabilistic factor graphs and GTSAM incremental solvers, achieving ~10 cm localization error without LiDAR in tennis-court settings at 160 FPS. Developed a pseudo-data generation pipeline based on ControlNet for line segmentation model training. Draft for RSS 2026.
Learning Dynamics Models for Objects from Real-World Camera Data
Combined optimization-based probabilistic inference with deep learning to jointly learn a factor-graph estimator and a learned predictor. Used roto-translational invariant representations to improve sample efficiency for surface bounce and aerodynamics modeling. Improved 86.7% over LSTM and 64.8% over diffusion-model baselines on real-world ping-pong and tennis setups. Published at ICRA 2024.
Multi-Camera Ball Localization and Prediction with Human Pose
Developed an asynchronous method for estimating fast-moving ball states using multi-camera observations and computer vision-based human pose detection. Achieved 63.6% improvement over EKF baselines using factor graphs with ~5 ms real-time inference via GTSAM. Published at ICRA 2025.
