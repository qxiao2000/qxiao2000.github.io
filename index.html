I am a PhD candidate in Robotics at Georgia Tech, advised by Prof. Matthew Gombolay. My research develops probabilistic state estimation and factor-graph inference methods for real-time robotic perception and prediction.

I build end-to-end systems that combine geometric vision, probabilistic inference, and modern learning to track and predict fast-moving objects from multiple cameras. My work has been deployed on tennis-playing robots, autonomous vehicles, and construction robots, with publications at ICRA and a draft for RSS 2026.

Research

My work sits at the intersection of probabilistic inference, geometric vision, and real-time systems:

Factor-Graph State Estimation Multi-Camera Tracking Dynamics Prediction GTSAM / Incremental Solvers ROS / ROS2
Agile Robot Localization with Multi-Camera Line Landmarks Draft · RSS 2026

An asynchronous pose estimator using probabilistic factor graphs and GTSAM incremental solvers achieves ~10 cm localization error without LiDAR in tennis-court settings at 160 FPS. Also developed a ControlNet-based pseudo-data generation pipeline to augment training of line segmentation models.

Learning Ball Dynamics with Differentiable Factor Graphs ICRA 2025

A framework that jointly learns a factor-graph estimator and a neural predictor using roto-translational invariant representations. Improves 86.7% over LSTM and 64.8% over diffusion-model baselines on real-world ping-pong and tennis dynamics.

Multi-Camera Ball Tracking with Human Pose and Factor Graphs ICRA 2024

An asynchronous multi-camera method for estimating fast-moving ball states that achieves 63.6% prediction improvement over EKF baselines, with ~5 ms real-time inference via GTSAM incremental solvers.

Cuboid Auto-Labeling with Learnable Factor Graphs Stack AV · Summer 2025

Structured probabilistic graphical models for cuboid distributions conditioned on camera and LiDAR observations. Improved recall from ~50% to ~90% while maintaining ~90% precision.
